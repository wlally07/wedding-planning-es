Types of Wedding Planning

Professional Planner or DIY?

Every couple is different. Whether you choose to work with a professional wedding planner or take a hands-on approach, understanding the benefits of each helps you make the right decision for your celebration.

Professional Wedding Planning

A professional wedding planner brings experience, industry connections and structured project management to your wedding day. For couples planning a destination wedding in Spain, a local planner offers invaluable knowledge of venues, suppliers, regulations and cultural expectations that would otherwise take months to research independently.

Professional planners manage every detail from initial concept through to the final farewell â€” handling supplier negotiations, contract reviews, budget tracking, timeline creation and on-the-day coordination. They anticipate problems before they arise, manage logistics across multiple vendors simultaneously, and ensure your vision is delivered exactly as planned. For international couples unfamiliar with Spanish wedding customs, supplier expectations and local regulations, this expertise removes significant risk and allows you to enjoy the planning process rather than feeling overwhelmed by it.

Access to established networks of trusted, vetted wedding suppliers across Spain
Expert budget management that often saves more than the planner's fee through supplier negotiations
Local knowledge of Spanish venues, regulations, marriage paperwork and cultural traditions
Complete day-of coordination ensuring every element runs to schedule without stress
Significant reduction in planning stress, allowing you to enjoy the engagement and the day itself

DIY Wedding Planning

DIY wedding planning gives you complete creative control over every aspect of your celebration. For couples who enjoy project management, have strong organisational skills and want a deeply personal touch throughout the planning process, managing your own wedding can be an incredibly rewarding experience that brings you closer together as a couple.

Planning your own wedding in Spain requires dedicated research into local venues, supplier availability, legal requirements for marriage, and logistical considerations such as guest transport and accommodation. Many couples combine a DIY approach with selective professional support â€” perhaps handling the creative vision themselves while hiring a day-of coordinator to manage the logistics. With the right planning tools, checklists and timelines, a self-planned wedding can be just as polished and memorable as one managed by a professional, while reflecting your personality in every detail from start to finish.

Full creative control over every decision, detail and design element of your wedding
Potential cost savings by managing vendor relationships and negotiations directly
A deeply personal planning journey that many couples find strengthens their partnership
Flexibility to mix professional support with self-managed elements to suit your style
Complete transparency and direct communication with every supplier and venue
